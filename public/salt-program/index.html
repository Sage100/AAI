The SALT Program

AAI administers the SALT (Shared Accountability, Lending & Teaching) program in Nigeria, combining biblical teaching with financial discipline to strengthen families and communities.

Program pillars Give to SALT Nigeria

Core components

  • Savings Groups Members meet weekly, contribute to a shared fund, and receive discipleship-driven business coaching. The pooled savings create rotating access to capital while encouraging accountability and stewardship.
  • Vocational Training (LHVS) Living Hope Vocational School offers core trades highlighted by SALT Nigeria: carpentry, tailoring, bagmaking, and shoemaking. Hands-on skills pair with biblical foundations to launch sustainable livelihoods.
  • Youth Savings Groups Youth cohorts learn disciplined saving, business basics, and Christ-centered accountability, mirroring the approach used in adult Savings Groups while preparing the next generation.
  • Business Teaching Practical instruction on pricing, record keeping, customer service, and planning helps participants apply principled business practices to grow responsibly.
  • Community Accountability Facilitators mentor group leaders, track growth, and reinforce faithful repayment, ensuring integrity and stability within each cluster.

How AAI serves Nigeria

AAI is the locally registered entity that coordinates SALT activities, onboards new Savings Groups and Youth Savings Groups, and aligns reporting with Brethren Hope for the Nations (the U.S. 501(c)(3) overseeing the work) and Christian Aid Ministries. This alignment keeps Nigerian operations transparent and accountable.

Program materials, training cohorts, and donor communication mirror the approach described on the SALT Nigeria website, ensuring a consistent experience for churches, community leaders, and government partners across Plateau, Borno, Bauchi, and neighboring states.
